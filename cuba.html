Brief History of Boxing
Boxing was believed to be started around 3000 BC in ancient Olympic games, where leather thongs (A narrow strip of leather) were wrapped around the participants hand to protect them. Roman’s later changed the nature of the sport by adding gloves stubbed with metal this then led to one person dying so the other could win the match, this practice came to an end with the fall of the roman empire. The sport was then revived in 17th century England where the sport began to modernize by adding weight classes and making the sport humane once more. The sport was later carried over to the Olympics in 1904.
Best and worst Olympic performance
Cuba’s best Olympic performance was at the start of the past thirty years at the 1992 Olympics where they recorded a total of eleven (11) bronze medal, five (5) silver medal and an extraordinary thirteen (13) gold medals. The worst recorded performance for Cuba for the past thirty years is their 2016 Rio de Janeiro performance
Individual that is excelling

Out of the 191 wins he knocked someone out an outstanding 32 times. He won an Olympic medal for Cuba in the 2016 Rio de Janeiro games in brazil. He won the heavyweight world title in 2011, 2015 and 2019.
