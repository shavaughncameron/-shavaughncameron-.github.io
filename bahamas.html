Bahamas Olympic record for the past thirty years
 
Introduction
Bahamas has a total of 19 medals in the Olympic games, with their first Olympic appearance being in London 1948 Olympic. Bahamas have gathered a massive three (3) gold medals, five (5) silver medals and eleven (11) bronze medal. Bahamas track and field achievement for the past 30 years: The first Olympic medal for this period was from Frank Rutherford in the men’s triple jump. The next Olympic they would see success once more in the Olympics with a silver medal in the women’s 4 x100 metres relay. The 2000 Olympics in Sydney came along, giving them their most successful run in the Olympics to date, they would pick up a whopping 3 Olympic medal for this year 3 Gold and a Bronze medal. They then followed that up with two medals in the 2004 Olympics in Athens Receiving a gold and a bronze medal. The coming 2008 Olympics would see a similar performance but this time only receiving a silver and a bronze medal, they then proceeded to get one gold medal in the London 2012 Olympics and gold and bronze in the 2016 Olympics in Rio de Janeiro, brazil.
History of track and field:
Track and field was believed to be dated back as far as the ninth or tenth century. The sport began to be developed with the Olympic further introducing marathon, relay and other variations to the sport in the modern games.
