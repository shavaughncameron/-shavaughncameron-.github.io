Jamaica’s Olympic record for the past 30 years
 
Introducion:
Jamaica has garnered a to a total of 78 Olympic medals since its first participation in 1960. For the past 30 years we have taken part in seven (7) Olympic event, in those Olympics we have had a grand total of 53 track and field medal in that period.
History of track and field:
Track and field was believed to be dated back as far as the ninth or tenth century. However, the first official track and field event that was recorded was the first Olympics in Olympia, this event is still carried through to modern era. The sport began to be developed with the Olympic further introducing marathon, relay and other variations to the sport in the modern games.

Jamaica’s best Olympic performance:
in the Olympic was the 2008 Olympics the team gathered a total of eleven medals; the same amount as London 2012 Olympics but the collection of awards was greater. The team received a total of five (5) gold medals, four (4) silver medals and two (2) bronze medals.

Jamaica’s worst Olympic performance:
Is the Olympic game in Barcelona the year 1992. They gathered a total of only four (4) medals, three (3) silver and a bronze medal; their worst performance in the past thirty years.

Track and field cannot be mentioned without talking about the great Usain St. Leo Bolt, an absolute legend in Jamaica’s track and field he is currently the fastest 100m world record holder. At an advantageous and controversial height of 1.96 metres he is able to double the length of his opponents stride with just one stride, even though his height aided him to these amazing things he was considered too tall for sprint events with persons thinking that his height would affect his performance. He has a total of six (6) Olympic Gold medal which is evenly divided between 100m and 200m sprint. He was born on August 21, 1986. At the age of just fifteen Usain Bolt took part in the world junior championships held at the Jamaica’s in 2002 at the national stadium in Kingston, Jamaica, at that event he won a goal medal being the youngest male junior champion in any event. The following year bolt shattered the record for the junior 200-metre World record in 20.13 seconds. In the coming years bolt would suffer some loss; pulling his hamstring in 2004 the Olympics and losing a world championship final in 2005. For the following years he proceeded to break multiple records.
Current superstar in the area: Shelly-Ann Fraser Pryce, born on December 7, 1986.
